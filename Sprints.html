Sprints

Sprints are the deliverables for this course. In the computer games industry, these correspond to milestones and successful completion of a milestone allows your game studio to get paid by their publisher. There are multiple parts to the later sprints (2-5). The implementations emphasize what you delivered - the product. Sprints 3-5 carry an additional part - response to feedback on the prior sprint. This part emphasizes process - how your team integrated feedback from the prior sprint. The response to feedback is also graded and affects your grade.
  • Individual based
    • Sprint 0 - tool setup (Visual Studio and MonoGame); sprite drawing and user input with a focus on using interfaces
    • Sprint 1 - team project tool usage (task tracking and source code control)
  • Team based
    • Sprint 2 - Game Objects and Sprites
    • Sprint 3 - Level as segmented screens and collision handling
    • Sprint 4 - Finished framework and first level
    • Sprint 5 - New features of your choice

Team sprint grading rubric: Team sprints are graded on Functionality, Documentation & Project Management, Code Quality, and Customer Interaction. See also:Notes on grading of sprints

Team evaluation spreadsheet: You can find links to this spreadsheet on the schedule page directly after the due dates for sprints 2-5. Fill it out honestly. It is not shared with other students. It provieds direct feedback to the instructor about team dynamics. If you do not fill it out, you might recieve a zero for the sprint. It will be updated after teams are formed.
